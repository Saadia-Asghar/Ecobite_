# EcoBite Business Model Canvas (Idea Stage)

## 1. Customer Segments (Who is this for?)
*   **Donors:** Restaurants, Cafes, Bakeries, Event Caterers, Supermarkets.
*   **Recipients:** NGOs, Food Banks, Orphanages, Animal Shelters (for non-human grade food).
*   **Partners:** Fertilizer companies (for spoiled waste).

## 2. Value Propositions (Why use EcoBite?)
*   **For Donors:** Zero-hassle food disposal, tax benefits/CSR credits, reduced waste disposal costs.
*   **For Recipients:** Free, reliable source of food; real-time alerts when food is available.
*   **Core Value:** A seamless tech platform connecting surplus food to those in need instantly.

## 3. Channels (How do they find you?)
*   EcoBite Mobile App & Web Dashboard.
*   Direct partnerships with Restaurant Associations.
*   Social Media & Community Outreach programs.

## 4. Customer Relationships (How do you keep them?)
*   **Automated Matching:** Donors don't need to call around; the app does it.
*   **Impact Reports:** Monthly dashboards showing KGs saved and meals served (gamification).
*   **Verification System:** Trust badges for verified NGOs.

## 5. Revenue Streams (How will you sustain this?)
*   **Corporate CSR Sponsorships:** Companies pay to sponsor the platform.
*   **Premium Analytics (Future):** Data insights for large restaurant chains on waste patterns.
*   **Grants & Awards:** Funding from sustainability organizations (e.g., Microsoft Imagine Cup).

## 6. Key Resources (What do you need?)
*   **Technology Stack:** The EcoBite App (React/Node.js) & Azure Cloud infrastructure.
*   **User Base:** Active network of registered NGOs and Restaurants.
*   **Data:** Azure AI Vision algorithms for food quality detection.

## 7. Key Activities (What are you doing daily?)
*   **Platform Development:** Coding and maintaining the app.
*   **Onboarding:** Verifying NGOs and signing up restaurants.
*   **Logistics Coordination:** Facilitating the connection (even if you don't own trucks).

## 8. Key Partnerships (Who helps you?)
*   **NGO Networks:** To redistribute food.
*   **Tech Partners:** Microsoft (Azure credits).
*   **Local Government:** For food safety guidelines and support.

## 9. Cost Structure (What are you spending money on?)
*   **Server/Cloud Costs:** Hosting and AI API usage.
*   **Marketing/Outreach:** Getting the first 50 restaurants on board.
*   **Development:** Time/cost for building the software.
